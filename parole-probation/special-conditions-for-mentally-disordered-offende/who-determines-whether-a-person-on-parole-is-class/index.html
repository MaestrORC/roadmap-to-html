Who determines whether a person on parole is classified as an MDO, and what is the process for making this decision?

There are several steps to the MDO determination process by CDCR:

STEP 1: First, CDCR mental health staff and a DSH psychologist or psychiatrist must screen you for classification as an MDO while you‚Äôre in prison.[495]

STEP 2: If both agree that you qualify as an MDO, the CDCR will send certification papers to the Board of Parole Hearings (BPH) stating that you have been found to be an MDO.[496]

STEP 3: The BPH then notifies the prisoner that he or she will have to undergo DSH inpatient treatment as a condition of parole.[497] The BPH also MUST notify the prisoner of his or her right to challenge the MDO finding.[498]
