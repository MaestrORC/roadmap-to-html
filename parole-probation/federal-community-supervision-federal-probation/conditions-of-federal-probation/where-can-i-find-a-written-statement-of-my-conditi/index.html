Where can I find a written statement of my conditions of federal probation?

The judge must direct the probation officer to provide you with a written statement that explains all the conditions you must follow, and is clear and specific enough to guide your conduct under supervision.[887]

The conditions of supervision are also listed on the “Judgment in a Criminal Case” produced by the court after your sentencing hearing. Ask your attorney or the Clerk of the Court where you were sentenced for a copy of this document so that you can be sure of your obligations to the Court. Most judges will not excuse your failure to comply with the conditions of supervision simply because you did not receive a copy of the conditions.[888]

  1. 887

    18 U.S.C. §§ 3563, 3583. ↑

  2. 888

    This is because the Judge also reads all of the conditions to you at your sentencing hearing. ↑
