What is a tribal ID card and why would I need it?

Tribal identification (ID) cards are issued by tribes as proof of your enrollment and membership in the tribe.

A tribal ID card is a valid form of government-issued photo identification in many places (though those places are often unaware of this). For example, a tribal ID card is valid at federal buildings, airports, and banks. It is also proof of eligibility for certain services such as the federal Indian Health Service; it allows you to opt out of Covered California (meaning, you can get an exemption from the tax penalty under ‚ÄúObamacare‚Äù);[234] and it can be used to prove your identification for the CA LifeLine cell phone program (read more on PG.¬†551).[235]
