Who is eligible for a U.S. Passport?

To be eligible for a U.S. Passport, you must:[199]

    Be a U.S. citizen or U.S. National;
    Provide a Social Security Number, proof of citizenship, and proof of identity;
    NOT be currently ‚Äúunder sentence‚Äù (incarcerated, on probation, or on parole) for any federal or state drug felony committed while using a passport or crossing international borders (drug trafficking);[200]
    NOT have a conviction for sex trafficking;[201]
    NOT be under a court order or sentence condition forbidding you from leaving the country‚Äîfor example, if your conditions of parole, probation, or some other type of supervision forbid you from leaving the U.S.,[202] you will need to ask your supervising officer or the court for permission to get a U.S. Passport or to change the conditions;
    Get permission from your supervising officer if you are under any form of supervision but are allowed to leave the country (See the IMPORTANT NOTE about getting permission from a supervising officer below);
    NOT have any state or federal warrants out for your arrest;[203]
    NOT owe $2,500 or more in child support.[204] For more information about paying off your child support debt, go to the COURT-ORDERED DEBT CHAPTER, beginning on PG.¬†717.

How do I apply for a U.S. Passport?

It depends on your situation. Some people can apply by mail. Others are required to apply in person.
