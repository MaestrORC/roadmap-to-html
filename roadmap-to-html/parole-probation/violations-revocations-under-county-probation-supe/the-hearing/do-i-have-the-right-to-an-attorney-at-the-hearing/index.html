Do I have the right to an attorney at the hearing?

Yes.[720] Before you waive the requirement to personally appear at the hearing or before you accept an offer of modification of your probation conditions, you should be informed that you have the right to an attorney, and if you cannot afford one, you have the right to a free attorney provided by the court.[721]

If you waive the right to an attorney, this waiver must be in writing.[722] Again, we suggest that you speak with an attorney or public defender prior to waiving your right to a revocation hearing.
