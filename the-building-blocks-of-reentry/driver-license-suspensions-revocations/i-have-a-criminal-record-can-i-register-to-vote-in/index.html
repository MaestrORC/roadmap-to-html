I have a criminal record. Can I register to vote in California?

It depends.[241] A MISDEMEANOR conviction never affects your voting rights in California. If you have only misdemeanor(s) on your record, you still have your voting rights! A FELONY conviction could cause you to lose your voting rights in California‚ÄîBUT ONLY while you are in prison for a felony; in jail waiting to be transferred to prison for a felony; in jail serving a ‚ÄúRealignment‚Äù felony sentence; and/or on state parole (not probation, PRCS, or Mandatory Supervision!).[242] Your right to vote is automatically restored after one of those ends. Note: If you were found mentally ill, you only lose your right to vote during the time you were involuntarily committed.[243]

See the chart on PG.¬†74 to better understand how your criminal record and supervision status will affect your voting rights.
