If I am found to be an MDO, and I want to be seen as an outpatient, is that possible?

It might be possible. The normal rule is that if you are found to be an MDO on parole, you must be placed in inpatient treatment (confined to a state hospital to live and receive mental health treatment)‚Äîunless the Department of State Hospitals (DSH) finds that you can be treated safely as an outpatient.[502]

What is the ‚ÄúDepartment of State Hospitals (DSH)‚Äù?

The DSH was created in 2012 to take over the functions of the now-defunct Department of Mental Health (DMH). DSH oversees inpatient mental health treatment facilities in California.

However, after 60 days in DSH custody, you can request a hearing in front of a BPH commissioner to ask for outpatient status as an MDO (where you live in the community but go to a mental health hospital for treatment).[503] At the hearing, the DSH must show by ‚Äúa preponderance of the evidence‚Äù (that it is more likely than not) that you require inpatient treatment.[504] At this hearing before the BPH, you have the right to a free appointed attorney (called a Panel Attorney)[505] and the appointment of two independent evaluators, and you may appeal the decision in County Superior Court (see below for more information).[506]
